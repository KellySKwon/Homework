Correlations Discovered Between Health Risk and Poverty

There's a positive correlation between Smoking and Poverty. States that have higher poverty tend to have more smokers in the population. However, this is not the case when plotting Smoking vs Age. Smoking tends to be among ages mid-30s to early-40s among the states.

Healthcare and Smoking follows the same trends with Poverty and Age. Although there's many facets to what is considered lacking healthcare (access to hospital such as transportation, insurance affordability, etc.) it's insightful to know that smoking prevention maybe one avenue to better health outcomes for those who are in poverty.
